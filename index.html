Tenses

Simple present tense: It is used to indicate the current action or an action that takes place regularly.
Formula: Subject + Verb in the base form/third person plural form + the rest of the sentence

Person Formula Example
I I + verb I play soccer.
You You + verb You eat apples.
He He + verb + s He reads books.
She She + verb + s She dances ballet.
It It + verb + s It rains often.
We We + verb We do homework.
They They + verb They live nearby.

Simple past tense: The simple past tense shows that you are talking about something that has already happened.
Formula: Subject + Verb in the past form (base form of the verb + ed/d for regular verbs or past tense form of the irregular verbs)

Person Formula Example
I I + verb (past form) I played soccer.
You You + verb (past form) You watched TV.
He He + verb (past form) He ate an apple.
She She + verb (past form) She danced ballet.
It It + verb (past form) It rained often.
We We + verb (past form) We did homework.
They They + verb (past form) They lived nearby.

Simple future tense: The simple future tense is a tense that is used to represent an action that takes place later.
Formula: Subject + Helping verb (will) + base form of the verb + the rest of the sentence

Person Formula Example
I I will + verb I will play soccer.
You You will + verb You will eat lunch.
He He will + verb He will read a book.
She She will + verb She will dance ballet.
It It will + verb It will rain tomorrow.
We We will + verb We will do our homework.
They They will + verb They will visit the park.

Words that I learnt from the story

Equipment: The tools, machines, or other items needed for a particular purpose or activity.
Sentence: The photographer set up his camera and lighting equipment for the photo shoot.

Frustrated: Feeling annoyed or upset because you cannot achieve something or because something is blocking your progress.
Sentence: She felt frustrated when her computer froze in the middle of her work.

Fuming: Feeling or showing intense anger.
Sentence: He was fuming when he found out someone had scratched his car.
